# Timeline & Keyframes

**The timeline is where your drawing becomes an animation — it controls when each element appears, how long it draws, and how it moves.** Whiteboard Video Maker gives you both a simple timeline and finer keyframe control.

## The timeline

Each element occupies a track on the timeline. Drag to set:

- **Start time** — when the element begins to appear.
- **Duration** — how long it takes to draw or reveal.

Play the whole sequence from the preview to feel the pacing.

## Draw order

The **draw order** decides the sequence in which the hand reveals elements. Reorder it so your story unfolds logically — title first, then the supporting shapes, then the punchline.

## Keyframes

For motion beyond a simple reveal, add **keyframes** to an element to animate:

- Position (move across the board)
- Scale (grow / shrink)
- Opacity (fade in / out)
- Rotation

Between keyframes the app interpolates the values. Use the **animation curves** editor to shape the easing (e.g. ease-in/out) rather than linear motion.

## The drawing hand

A follow-along **hand animation** can trace strokes as they are drawn, reinforcing the "someone is drawing this" effect. It follows the draw order and the timing you set.

## Transitions & timing helpers

- **Transitions** — move between scenes/segments with a transition effect.
- **Smart timing** — apply timing presets so reveals feel natural without hand-tuning every element.
- **Beat markers** — align reveals to a beat for rhythmic, music-synced animations.
- **Loop markers** — mark a segment as loopable.
- **Auto-layout** — arrange elements automatically.

## Synced narration

Text and elements can be synced to narration so captions or labels appear exactly when they are spoken. See [Audio & narration](./audio-narration.md).
