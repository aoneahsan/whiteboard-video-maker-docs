# Whiteboard Video Maker

**Whiteboard Video Maker is a free, browser- and mobile-based application for creating hand-drawn whiteboard-style animation videos.** You draw shapes, text, and images with sketch-style strokes, arrange them on a layered timeline, optionally add narration, and export the result as a WebM video or animated GIF — entirely in the browser, with no subscription and no watermark.

The live app is at **[wbamai.aoneahsan.com](https://wbamai.aoneahsan.com)**. These docs explain what it does, how to use it, and where its limits are.

## Who it is for

- **Teachers and trainers** building explainer content
- **YouTube / TikTok creators** producing educational shorts
- **Marketers** making product walkthroughs
- **Students** animating concepts for assignments
- **Hobbyists** experimenting with hand-drawn motion design

## What makes it different

| | Whiteboard Video Maker |
|---|---|
| **Cost** | Free — no subscription, no usage caps, no watermark |
| **Where it runs** | Any modern browser, plus native Android and iOS apps |
| **Rendering** | Client-side — your frames are rendered and encoded in the browser |
| **Export** | WebM video (VP9/Opus) and animated GIF |
| **Look** | Authentic hand-drawn strokes via [RoughJS](https://roughjs.com) |
| **Narration** | Built-in text-to-speech (Web Speech API) and voice recording |
| **Account** | Optional — sign in with Google to save and sync projects |

## How it works, in one minute

1. Open the editor and pick a **board style** (whiteboard, blackboard, glassboard, or greenscreen).
2. Add elements with the **drawing tools** — pen, shapes, arrows, text, and images.
3. Arrange them on the **timeline** and tune per-element **keyframes** and draw order.
4. Optionally add **narration** (text-to-speech or your own recording) and audio.
5. **Export** to WebM or GIF — the render happens in your browser.

## Honest limits

So you know what to expect before you start:

- **Export is WebM and animated GIF, not MP4.** WebM plays in every modern browser and on most platforms; if you specifically need MP4 you would convert the WebM afterward with a separate tool.
- **Rendering uses your device.** Long, high-resolution projects take longer to encode on slower machines because there is no cloud render farm — that is the trade-off for being free and private.
- **Text-to-speech voices come from your browser/OS.** Available voices and quality vary by platform.
- **Saving and cross-device sync require signing in.** Signed-out work lives only in the current session.
