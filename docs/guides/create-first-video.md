# Create Your First Video

**This guide walks through a complete whiteboard animation end-to-end — plan, draw, sequence, narrate, preview, and export.** Allow about 15–20 minutes for a short (15–30 second) clip the first time.

## Step 1 — Plan a tiny script

Keep it small. Write one or two sentences and the 3–4 visuals that illustrate them. A clear plan makes the timeline step trivial.

## Step 2 — Open the editor and pick a board

Open **[wbamai.aoneahsan.com/editor](https://wbamai.aoneahsan.com/editor)** and choose a [board style](../features/board-styles.md). Whiteboard is the safest default.

## Step 3 — Add a title

Use the text tool to add your headline. Set a font and a reveal effect (typewriter reads well for titles). Place it near the top.

## Step 4 — Add supporting visuals

Add 3–4 elements that support the script — shapes, arrows, an icon from the [library](../features/assets-libraries.md), or an imported image. Don't overcrowd the board.

## Step 5 — Set the draw order and timing

On the [timeline](../features/timeline-keyframes.md):

1. Order elements so they appear in narrative sequence.
2. Set each element's start time and draw duration.
3. Add keyframes if anything needs to move, grow, or fade.

## Step 6 — Add narration

Open the audio panel and either generate [text-to-speech](../features/audio-narration.md) from your script or record your voice. Align the narration so words match the visuals.

## Step 7 — Preview

Play the full animation in the preview. Watch for:

- Text that reveals too fast or too slow
- Elements that overlap awkwardly
- Narration that drifts out of sync

Adjust timing until it feels right.

## Step 8 — Export

Open the export dialog, choose a resolution and format, and render. See [Export to WebM or GIF](./export-webm-gif.md) for the trade-offs.

## Step 9 — Save it (optional)

[Sign in](./save-and-sync-projects.md) to save the project so you can refine it later or reuse it as a template.

## Tips for good first results

- **Shorter is better.** A tight 15-second clip beats a rambling minute.
- **High contrast.** Keep text readable against the board.
- **One idea per reveal.** Let each element land before the next appears.
